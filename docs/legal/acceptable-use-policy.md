## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines what is and is not permitted when
using Patchwork. It supplements the
[Terms of Service](./terms-of-service.md) and
[Community Guidelines](./community-guidelines.md). By using Patchwork you
agree to comply with this policy.

## 2. Permitted Uses

Patchwork is designed for mutual aid coordination. Permitted uses include:

- Posting genuine requests for help or assistance.
- Offering help, resources, or skills to others in your community.
- Discovering and responding to nearby aid requests.
- Coordinating volunteer activities and community resource sharing.
- Communicating with other users about aid coordination through the platform's
  messaging features.
- Providing feedback on completed aid interactions.
- Managing community resource directory listings for organisations.

## 3. Prohibited Uses

The following uses are prohibited. Violations may result in enforcement
actions as described in the
[Community Guidelines](./community-guidelines.md).

### 3.1 Fraud and Deception

- Posting fraudulent, misleading, or deceptive aid requests or offers.
- Soliciting donations or financial contributions under false pretences.
- Impersonating another person, organisation, or verification status.
- Creating fake accounts or using stolen credentials.

### 3.2 Commercial Activity

- Using Patchwork for commercial advertising, marketing, or sales.
- Promoting paid services disguised as mutual aid offers.
- Multi-level marketing, pyramid schemes, or similar commercial solicitation.

### 3.3 Harmful Content

- Posting content that harasses, threatens, or intimidates others.
- Posting discriminatory content targeting protected characteristics.
- Posting content that promotes violence, self-harm, or illegal activity.
- Posting content that exploits or endangers children.
- Distributing malware or malicious links.

### 3.4 Privacy Violations

- Sharing another user's personal information without their consent.
- Attempting to de-anonymise or locate users who have chosen privacy controls.
- Circumventing geo-privacy features or minimum precision controls.

### 3.5 Platform Manipulation

- Manipulating discovery, ranking, or matching algorithms.
- Artificially inflating report counts or verification scores.
- Submitting false moderation reports.
- Circumventing enforcement actions (including creating new accounts after a
  ban).

### 3.6 Circumventing Safety

- Attempting to bypass moderation tools, content filters, or safety features.
- Exploiting platform vulnerabilities or security weaknesses.
- Interfering with the platform's operation or availability.
- Tampering with audit trails or moderation records.

## 4. Spam

The following are considered spam and are prohibited:

- Posting identical or substantially similar content repeatedly.
- Sending unsolicited bulk messages.
- Posting content unrelated to mutual aid coordination.
- Using the platform primarily to drive traffic to external services.

## 5. Rate Limits and Automated Access

### 5.1 Rate Limits

Patchwork enforces rate limits to ensure fair access and platform stability.
Users who exceed rate limits may experience temporary restrictions on their
ability to post, message, or perform certain actions.

### 5.2 Automated Access

- **Bots and scripts** are not permitted to create accounts, post content, or
  send messages without prior written authorisation.
- **API access** for legitimate integrations must comply with published API
  rate limits and usage guidelines.
- **Scraping** user data, content, or platform resources is prohibited.
- Authorised automated access must identify itself clearly and must not
  impersonate human users.

### 5.3 Research Access

Researchers seeking to study the platform or its data must contact us in
advance to discuss access terms and ethical review requirements.

## 6. Consequences of Violation

Violations of this AUP are handled through the enforcement framework
described in the [Community Guidelines](./community-guidelines.md):

1. **Warning** -- Written notice for first-time or minor violations.
2. **Content action** -- Delisting or suspension of specific content.
3. **Account restriction** -- Temporary or permanent suspension of account
   access.

The severity of enforcement depends on:

- The nature and severity of the violation.
- Whether the violation was intentional or negligent.
- The user's prior enforcement history.
- The impact on other users or the platform.

Emergency removal applies for violations that pose an immediate safety risk
(see [Emergency Content Removal SOP](../operations/moderation-sops.md)).
