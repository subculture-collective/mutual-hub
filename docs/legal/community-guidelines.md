## 1. Mission Statement

Patchwork exists to make mutual aid coordination accessible, transparent, and
trustworthy. We believe communities are strongest when people can ask for help
and offer help freely, without barriers. These guidelines exist to protect that
mission and to keep Patchwork a safe, respectful space for everyone.

## 2. Our Values

- **Solidarity** -- We support each other because we share a community, not
  because of charity or transaction.
- **Transparency** -- Visible seams, not black boxes. Moderation actions are
  logged and auditable.
- **Good faith** -- We assume the best of each other while maintaining
  appropriate safeguards.
- **Dignity** -- Every person on this platform deserves respect, regardless of
  their circumstances.

## 3. Expected Behaviour

All users are expected to:

- **Act in good faith.** Post genuine requests and offers. Follow through on
  commitments where possible.
- **Be respectful.** Treat every user with courtesy and empathy. Disagreements
  are fine; personal attacks are not.
- **Be honest.** Provide accurate information about yourself, your needs, and
  your capacity to help.
- **Protect privacy.** Do not share another user's personal information,
  location, or contact details without their explicit consent.
- **Use appropriate channels.** Use the reporting and feedback tools for
  concerns rather than public call-outs.
- **Respect moderation decisions.** Use the appeal process if you disagree
  with a decision (see Section 7).

## 4. Prohibited Content and Behaviour

The following are not permitted on Patchwork:

### 4.1 Fraud and Deception

- Posting fraudulent aid requests or offers.
- Soliciting money, goods, or services under false pretences.
- Misrepresenting your identity, qualifications, or verification status.

### 4.2 Harassment and Abuse

- Targeted harassment, bullying, or intimidation of any user.
- Repeated unwanted contact after being asked to stop.
- Threats of violence, whether direct or implied.
- Doxxing or publishing another user's private information.

### 4.3 Discrimination

- Content or behaviour that demeans, excludes, or attacks individuals or
  groups based on race, ethnicity, national origin, gender, gender identity,
  sexual orientation, religion, disability, age, or any other protected
  characteristic.

### 4.4 Illegal Activity

- Using the platform to facilitate illegal activity of any kind.
- Distributing controlled substances or prohibited items.
- Content that violates intellectual property rights.

### 4.5 Safety Violations

- Content involving child exploitation or endangerment.
- Content promoting self-harm or suicide.
- Incitement to violence.

### 4.6 Spam and Manipulation

- Posting repetitive, unsolicited, or irrelevant content.
- Automated posting without authorisation.
- Attempting to manipulate platform rankings or discovery algorithms.
- Creating multiple accounts to circumvent enforcement actions.

### 4.7 Platform Abuse

- Attempting to circumvent moderation, safety, or privacy features.
- Exploiting platform vulnerabilities.
- Scraping or bulk-downloading user data without authorisation.

## 5. Enforcement Actions

When a violation is identified, the following actions may be taken by
moderators. These align with the moderation policy actions defined in the
platform's moderation system:

| Action                    | Description                                  | Severity   |
| ------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| **Warning**               | A written notice that specific behaviour violates guidelines. No content change. | Low |
| **Content delisting** (`delist`) | Content is removed from public discovery but preserved for audit review. | Medium |
| **Visibility suspension** (`suspend-visibility`) | Content is fully hidden from all users for serious policy violations. | High |
| **Account suspension**    | Temporary or permanent suspension of account access. | Severe |

### 5.1 Graduated Enforcement

Enforcement follows a graduated model:

1. **First violation (minor):** Warning with explanation of the specific
   guideline violated.
2. **Repeated or moderate violation:** Content delisting or visibility
   suspension, with written reason.
3. **Serious or repeated violation:** Account suspension (temporary or
   permanent).

### 5.2 Emergency Removal

Content that poses an immediate safety risk (threats of violence, child safety
concerns, doxxing, active fraud targeting vulnerable people) may be removed
immediately without following the graduated process. See
[Emergency Content Removal SOP](../operations/moderation-sops.md) for the
detailed procedure.

### 5.3 Audit Trail

Every enforcement action creates an immutable audit record containing the
action type, the actor, the timestamp, the reason, and the state change. This
ensures transparency and accountability in moderation.

## 6. Reporting

### 6.1 How to Report

If you encounter content or behaviour that violates these guidelines:

1. Use the **Report** function on the content or user profile.
2. Provide a clear description of the concern and select the relevant
   category.
3. You will receive an acknowledgement with a reference ID.

### 6.2 What Happens After a Report

1. The report enters the moderation queue for triage.
2. A moderator reviews the report within the target SLA (initial triage within
   4 hours).
3. The moderator takes action or escalates as appropriate.
4. You may receive a notification about the outcome, depending on the nature
   of the report.

### 6.3 False Reports

Submitting knowingly false reports is itself a violation of these guidelines
and may result in enforcement action against the reporter.

## 7. Appeal Process

### 7.1 Your Right to Appeal

If your content has been delisted or suspended, or if your account has been
restricted, you have the right to appeal the decision.

### 7.2 How to Appeal

1. Navigate to your account or the affected content and select "Appeal
   decision."
2. Provide a written explanation of why you believe the decision was
   incorrect (10 to 2000 characters).
3. Your appeal will be acknowledged immediately with an appeal ID.

### 7.3 Appeal Review

1. A lead moderator or admin reviews the appeal within 3 business days.
2. If additional information is needed, the appeal moves to "under review" and
   you will be notified.
3. The reviewer either upholds the original decision or reverses it.
4. Final decision target: 5 business days.

### 7.4 Escalation

If you disagree with the appeal outcome, you may escalate further:

1. **First escalation** -- Request review by a senior Trust & Safety reviewer.
   Target: 5 business days.
2. **Second escalation** -- The case is forwarded to the Platform Governance
   Board. Target: 15 business days.
3. **Final decision** -- The Governance Board's decision is final.

For full details, see
[Verification Appeals and Escalation Process](../operations/verification-appeals.md).

## 8. Moderator Conduct

Moderators are held to a higher standard:

- Moderators must act impartially and document reasons for every action.
- Moderators must not use their position to settle personal disputes.
- Moderator actions are logged in the audit trail and subject to review.
- Concerns about moderator conduct should be reported to a lead moderator or
  admin.

See [Moderation SOPs](../operations/moderation-sops.md) for the full
moderator operating procedures.

## 9. Changes to These Guidelines

will be communicated through the platform at least 30 days before taking
effect. All changes are recorded in the
